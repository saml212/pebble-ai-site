let them eat bytes.

I measure reasoning capacity inside language models by giving them matrix-valued tokens.

A matrix has rank. A vector does not. Rank is a differentiable structural property of a single representation, and it bounds how many reasoning paths a model can hold in superposition during inference-time compute. I test whether matrix rank correlates with the number of reasoning paths a continuous-reasoning model actually holds — a question the theoretical literature has asked and no empirical work has answered. Along the way I build structured representations for byte-level models because bytes are where the field is moving and they need something language-tokenizers have been providing as a crutch.

matrix-CODI complete  ·  findings being published

the through line

Three steps. If each works, the next one becomes possible.

Byte-level processing is where the field is moving and it has specific problems. Matrix-valued representations address those problems and give a structural observable no prior approach has. That observable makes a new kind of inference-time reasoning possible: machine-native, structured, measurable.

01 the problem

Byte-level processing has specific problems.

Byte-level models are where the field is going. Meta replaced Chameleon (a VQ-tokenized multimodal model) with BLT (byte-level, tokenizer-free). DINOv3 showed self-supervised visual learning without language supervision matches CLIP at scale. EvaByte, MambaByte, and MBLM have all demonstrated byte-level models that work. But bytes introduce three specific problems:

  • Extreme context length. Bytes are ~3.5× more numerous than subword tokens for the same text, and far more numerous for audio and video.
  • Computational efficiency. Standard O(L²) attention falls over at byte scale.
  • Bytes are hard to relate to each other. A single byte carries very little signal. Most of the meaning lives in the relationships between bytes in a window, and flat-vector models have to rediscover those relationships implicitly at every layer.

The third problem is the one I focus on.

02 the answer

Matrix-valued tokens carry relational structure in the representation itself.

A matrix-valued token representation carries relational structure directly in the token. Each token is a d × d matrix whose entries can encode learned pairwise interactions between bytes in a local window. The model no longer has to rediscover those relationships implicitly at every layer. In my experiments, outer-product matrix embeddings cut per-parameter perplexity by 11× at matched parameters against flat vectors — see findings / outer-product embedding.

Two properties of matrices matter for this:

  • Rank is a measurable, differentiable observable. It quantifies how many independent patterns a representation encodes. Rank can be computed from a single matrix; for a flat vector, it can only be estimated across an ensemble.
  • Structure provides inductive bias. Recent evidence at this compute regime favors structured representations (HELM hyperbolic LLM at billion-parameter scale, equivariant transformers at matched compute), and the structured-architecture side now has existence proofs in language modeling where none existed two years ago.

The contextualized matrix embedding is a specific realization of this. Instead of each token being a rank-1 outer product (the current baseline), each token's matrix encodes learned pairwise interactions across a 16-byte window. The embedding carries relational structure from the input layer.

03 what it unlocks

Matrix structure makes inference-time reasoning measurable.

Continuous-reasoning models like COCONUT (Meta 2024) and CODI (Meta 2025) are the closest thing to machine-native inference-time compute. They let the model "think" by generating latent representations before producing an output. The theoretical claim from the 2025 literature (Reasoning by Superposition, CoT2) is that these latent states hold multiple reasoning paths in superposition. The empirical problem: nobody has measured the structural property the claim describes. A 2026 rebuttal (Illusion of Superposition, arXiv 2604.06374) argues the superposition might be an artifact of how outputs are interpreted.

Matrix tokens make the measurement possible. If a matrix of rank r holds r linearly independent patterns by construction, then tracking rank during reasoning tracks the number of paths the model holds. The next experiment tests exactly this, and can settle the published dispute.

Supporting framing: Fedorenko et al. (Nature, 2024) dissociates the human language network from reasoning, math, and theory of mind — evidence that reasoning can run on representations that are not language-shaped.

no. 05  ·  april 14, 2026

Matrix-CODI is decorative: four flat curves and a linear-algebra reason

A fork of CODI with a 16×16 matrix bottleneck at each latent reasoning step. Four flat rank-k projection curves across two tasks, two distillation regimes, two thinker settings. A three-seed vanilla supervised fine-tune matches matrix-CODI within 0.26 points. The forward pass reads Z through w_down(Z.flatten()), a linear map whose Jacobian is constant in Z; no gradient can reward the singular structure. Nine architectural fixes all reduce to the same form under reshape. Adjudicates the Reasoning by Superposition vs Illusion of Superposition dispute at a mechanistic level. Read the research note →

Output mechanism shapes rank trajectory in matrix-valued refinement

Across three runs that share the Matrix Thinker backbone but vary in output mechanism, the direction of the rank trajectory tracks the output mechanism. Bilinear probes see rank rise across 8 iterations; vector-collapse and 3D matrix-product runs see rank fall. The runs are not FLOPs-matched and this is an observational single-seed finding, not a causal claim. Read the research note →

roadmap

Where this goes after matrix-CODI.

now →

Matrix-native from scratch on a rank-K task

The matrix-CODI negative result localized the failure to the linear-in-Z readout. The next experiment removes that readout entirely. A small (~10M parameter) fully matrix-native transformer — matrix Q/K/V with true matrix composition, no flatten anywhere in the forward pass, matrix LM head — trained from scratch on a synthetic task whose ground truth provably requires K independent scalars of state. If matrix structure is functional, this is where it shows.

next →

Contextualized matrix embeddings

Test four embedding designs: k-bigram outer products, conv encoder, local attention contextualization, and pairwise interaction matrices. The current rank-1 outer-product embedding is information-equivalent to storing two vectors. Higher-rank starting embeddings give the matrix meaningful structure from the input layer.

~30 H100-hours
next →

Byte-level JEPA with LeJEPA SIGReg

Machine-native representations from raw bytes, no tokenizer, no language scaffolding. Translates WavJEPA (raw audio waveforms) to byte streams with a conv byte patcher, using LeJEPA's sketched isotropic Gaussian regularization to prevent collapse. No published byte-level JEPA exists as of April 2026. Genuine gap to fill.

Hierarchical long-context byte model

Combines MBLM-style hierarchy (Mamba for long-range patch processing, transformer for local byte processing) with matrix-valued patch representations. Unlocks 1M+ byte context windows for the matrix architecture. Required infrastructure for multi-domain training.

scale →

Transfer learning across byte-level modalities

Train a single model on raw bytes from text, code, raw pixel images, and raw audio samples. Measure cross-domain transfer coefficients and representation alignment. Test whether matrix structure specifically enables transfer that flat-vector baselines can't match. Requires 10M+ parameters and a mixed-modality byte corpus, which has to be constructed first.

scale →

Inference-time matrix reasoning at competitive scale

Matrix-CODI at 10-50M parameters on real benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, ProsQA, MNNS). Where the toy-scale rank experiment tells if the phenomenon exists, this tells how much it matters when the model is actually competent. Published comparison against CODI, CoT2, CoLaR, and MarCos.

Synthetic reasoning datasets with exact frontier measurement

Generate a calibrated benchmark suite where the number of reasoning paths at each step is analytically computable (subset-sum variants, graph reachability, rule composition). This lets rank correlations be tested against ground truth rather than annotator-inferred step counts. Datasets released publicly.

stretch →

Fully matrix-native architecture at scale

HELM showed a fully hyperbolic billion-parameter LLM can match Euclidean baselines when every operation commits to the structured space. We'd replicate the approach with matrix-valued tokens: matrix attention, matrix FFN, matrix normalization, no flatten anywhere. Compute-intensive, only justified if the smaller experiments produce strong signal.

dead ends

Directions the evidence has ruled out.

Negative results are data. These are the specific hypotheses I tested and ruled out, which narrowed the current direction. Each one was a pre-registered experiment with a clear falsification criterion.

PHM learned algebraic structure

Parameterized Hypercomplex Multiplication layers were supposed to learn quaternion-like algebra. Instead they converge to nilpotent structure — the optimizer treats PHM as a low-rank factorization rather than a learned algebra. CliffordNet (2026) confirmed the same thing from the other direction: algebraic structure works when fixed, not when learned.

PonderNet halting at small scale

Adaptive halting mechanisms collapse to "always stop at step 1" below ~10M parameters. Expected steps converges to 1.0. Use fixed iteration counts or LoopFormer-style consistency training instead.

3D matrix attention

Computing per-pair matrix products as attention scores drives representations to collapse to rank 1 and produces worse predictions. Dead end confirmed across multiple runs and scales.

Cross-domain transfer (original framing)

The original "matrix structure enables cross-domain transfer" hypothesis was killed by six fatal attacks before any experiment ran. The question survives as a research direction but needed a sharper formulation and larger scale to be testable.

Thought interleaving at toy scale

CoCoMix-style thought interleaving mechanisms don't produce benefits below ~10M parameters. The mechanism works but the scale is insufficient to measure what it unlocks.

Learned byte segmentation at small scale

Learned segmentation boundaries consistently lose to fixed-stride segmentation at the scales I tested. The learning signal isn't strong enough below ~100M parameters. BLT works at 8B, so this may reverse at scale — but not within this compute regime.
