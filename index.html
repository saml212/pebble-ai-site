let them eat bytes.

We audit the structural claims continuous-reasoning models make, and build the byte-level representations they sit on top of.

A matrix has rank. A vector does not. Rank is a differentiable structural property of a single representation, and the 2025 theoretical literature argued it should bound how many reasoning paths a continuous-reasoning model holds in superposition during inference-time compute. We tested that claim directly with a matrix-valued variant of CODI and it failed — for a specific, publishable reason in linear algebra. Read the full audit. Alongside the audit we build structured representations for byte-level models because bytes are where the field is moving and they need something language-tokenizers have been providing as a crutch.

30 experiments on 8×H100 pods  ·  matrix-CODI completed, structural negative result  ·  read the audit

the through line

One investigation, three stages, each building on the previous.

Byte-level processing is where the field is moving, and it has specific problems. Matrix-valued representations address those problems and provide a structural observable no prior approach has. That observable makes a new kind of inference-time reasoning possible: machine-native, structured, measurable.

01 the problem

Byte-level processing has specific problems.

Byte-level models are where the field is going. Meta replaced Chameleon (a VQ-tokenized multimodal model) with BLT (byte-level, tokenizer-free). DINOv3 showed self-supervised visual learning without language supervision matches CLIP at scale. EvaByte, MambaByte, and MBLM have all demonstrated byte-level models that work. But bytes introduce three specific problems:

  • Extreme context length. Bytes are ~3.5× more numerous than subword tokens for the same text, and far more numerous for audio and video.
  • Computational efficiency. Standard O(L²) attention falls over at byte scale.
  • Bytes are hard to relate to each other. A single byte carries very little signal. Most of the meaning lives in the relationships between bytes in a window, and flat-vector models have to rediscover those relationships implicitly at every layer.

The third problem is the one we focus on.

02 the answer

Matrix-valued tokens carry relational structure in the representation itself.

A matrix-valued token representation carries relational structure directly in the token. Each token is a d × d matrix whose entries can encode learned pairwise interactions between bytes in a local window. The model no longer has to rediscover those relationships implicitly at every layer. In our experiments, outer-product matrix embeddings cut per-parameter perplexity by 11× at matched parameters against flat vectors — see findings / outer-product embedding.

Two properties of matrices matter for this:

  • Rank is a measurable, differentiable observable. It quantifies how many independent patterns a representation encodes. Rank can be computed from a single matrix; for a flat vector, it can only be estimated across an ensemble.
  • Structure provides inductive bias. Recent evidence at our compute regime favors structured representations (HELM hyperbolic LLM at billion-parameter scale, equivariant transformers at matched compute), and the structured-architecture side now has existence proofs in language modeling where none existed two years ago.

The contextualized matrix embedding is a specific realization of this. Instead of each token being a rank-1 outer product (our current baseline), each token's matrix encodes learned pairwise interactions across a 16-byte window. The embedding carries relational structure from the input layer.

03 what this makes auditable

Matrix structure makes the claims that continuous-reasoning models rest on auditable.

Continuous-reasoning models like COCONUT (Meta 2024) and CODI (Meta 2025) are the closest we have to machine-native inference-time compute. They let the model "think" by generating latent representations before producing an output. The theoretical claim from the 2025 literature (Reasoning by Superposition, CoT2) is that these latent states hold multiple reasoning paths in superposition, with capacity bounded by an embedding-dimension-shaped quantity. The 2026 empirical rebuttal — Rizvi-Martel et al., The Illusion of Superposition? (arXiv 2604.06374) — showed that, in fine-tuned COCONUT on ProsQA, removing the latent tokens entirely costs only 2.4 points of accuracy (99.0% → 96.6%). Fine-tuned latent reasoners don't use their latents.

We asked a narrower follow-up: could a matrix-valued latent use rank as a functional quantity, even in principle, inside a CODI-style architecture? Matrix tokens are the right probe because rank is computable from a single sample and the 2025 theory papers use rank-like language directly. We built it, ran it across two tasks and four training conditions, and the answer was no — for a specific linear-algebra reason. Any readout that reduces to a linear map on vec(Z) has a constant Jacobian with respect to Z, so the loss gradient cannot reward Z's singular structure. The result is published as Matrix-CODI is Decorative, and it closes off a family of architectural fixes without running them.

Supporting framing: Fedorenko et al. (Nature, 2024) dissociates the human language network from reasoning, math, and theory of mind — evidence that reasoning can run on representations that are not language-shaped. The negative matrix-CODI result tells us the next useful question is what continuous thoughts encode, via probing, rather than how much of some structural capacity they use.

complete  ·  negative result

Matrix-CODI is decorative: a structural audit of continuous reasoning bottlenecks.

A fork of CODI with a 16 × 16 matrix bottleneck at each latent reasoning step, trained on GSM8K-Aug and ProsQA. The rank-k projection ablation is flat in all four training conditions we tested, and there is a linear-algebra reason why. The contribution is not the empirical observation that the latents are decorative — Rizvi-Martel et al., The Illusion of Superposition? (arXiv 2604.06374, 2026) already established that empirically at a much stronger operating point than ours. The contribution is a structural mechanism that explains why no readout in the linear-in-Z family can use Z's rank, regardless of training procedure.

experiment status  ·  complete  ·  read the research note

A structural explanation for an empirical observation already in the literature.

What was already known. Rizvi-Martel et al., The Illusion of Superposition? [arXiv 2604.06374, 2026], showed empirically that fine-tuned COCONUT on ProsQA reaches 99.0% with latents and 96.6% without, against 85.3% for explicit chain-of-thought. The "latents are decorative in fine-tuned latent reasoners" finding is theirs. We do not reproduce it — our vanilla GPT-2 supervised fine-tune on ProsQA averages 81.77% across three seeds, roughly 15 points below their published baseline — and we do not claim the empirical finding as ours.

What this note adds. A structural argument that complements the Illusion paper. The forward pass in any matrix-valued bottleneck reads Z through w_down(Z.flatten()), a linear map whose Jacobian with respect to Z is a constant tensor independent of Z. The loss gradient therefore cannot reward Z's singular structure, and the model places its useful information in a single component and every downstream rank is decoration. Nine architectural fixes we designed to escape this — bilinear K-probes, quadratic Z Z⊤ readouts, spectral polynomial features, matrix-valued attention, an end-to-end matrix LM head, train-time rank constraints — all reduce to linear-in-Z under reshape and inherit the same rank-blindness. None reached a GPU, because the linear-algebra argument kills them in advance.

What our local experiments show. Inside our training pipeline: four flat rank-k ablation curves across two tasks, two distillation regimes, and two thinker settings; a vanilla GPT-2 SFT control that matches matrix-CODI within seed noise on ProsQA. Both matrix-CODI and our vanilla SFT sit ~15 points below the published state of the art on ProsQA and ~36 points below published CODI on GSM8K. The flat curves and the same-pipeline vanilla SFT match are real within our setup; none of the cross-setup empirical claims (on absolute ProsQA accuracy or on fine-tuned-latents-are-decorative in general) are ours.

The headline. The Illusion of Superposition paper asks "are the latents doing work?" and answers empirically "no." This note asks a narrower follow-up — "could a matrix-valued latent do work through its singular structure?" — and answers structurally "no, not under any linear-in-Z readout, by linear algebra." The two findings are complementary and independent: a behavioral test and a Jacobian calculation. Read the full note →

negative  ·  structural

Rank enrichment is a phenomenon not reported in prior work

With a bilinear output head, the effective rank of matrix token representations rises during iterative refinement (5.02 → 6.12). This runs counter to the prior literature's assumption that depth drives rank collapse. Not reported in any prior work. Read the research note →

novel

Output mechanism shapes rank trajectory in matrix-valued refinement

Across three runs that share the Matrix Thinker backbone but vary in output mechanism, the direction of the rank trajectory tracks the output mechanism. Bilinear probes see rank rise across 8 iterations; vector-collapse and 3D matrix-product runs see rank fall. The runs are not FLOPs-matched and this is an observational single-seed finding, not a causal claim. Read the research note →

observational

Outer-product matrix embeddings outperform flat vectors per parameter

At single-step processing, a matrix token produced by outer-product embedding beats a flat-vector baseline by 11× in perplexity at matched parameters. Reproduced across every configuration we tested. Read the research note →

proven

Matrix operations are 130× more parameter-efficient per layer

At d = 16, a matrix transformer layer uses 130× fewer parameters than a standard vector attention layer at matched capacity. This changes the parameter allocation of the whole model: where embeddings used to dominate, thinking layers can. Read the research note →

Contextualized matrix embeddings

Test four embedding designs: k-bigram outer products, conv encoder, local attention contextualization, and pairwise interaction matrices. The current rank-1 outer-product embedding is information-equivalent to storing two vectors. Higher-rank starting embeddings give the matrix meaningful structure from the input layer.

Fully matrix-native architecture at scale

HELM showed a fully hyperbolic billion-parameter LLM can match Euclidean baselines when every operation commits to the structured space. dead ends

Directions the evidence has ruled out.

Negative results are data. These are the specific hypotheses we tested and ruled out, which narrowed the current direction. Each one represents a pre-registered experiment with a clear falsification criterion.

PHM learned algebraic structure

Parameterized Hypercomplex Multiplication layers were supposed to learn quaternion-like algebra. Instead they converge to nilpotent structure — the optimizer treats PHM as a low-rank factorization rather than a learned algebra. CliffordNet (2026) confirmed the same thing from the other direction: algebraic structure works when fixed, not when learned.

PonderNet halting at small scale

Adaptive halting mechanisms collapse to "always stop at step 1" below ~10M parameters. Expected steps converges to 1.0. Use fixed iteration counts or LoopFormer-style consistency training instead.

3D matrix attention

Computing per-pair matrix products as attention scores drives representations to collapse to rank 1 and produces worse predictions. Dead end confirmed across multiple runs and scales.

Cross-domain transfer (original framing)

The original "matrix structure enables cross-domain transfer" hypothesis was killed by six fatal attacks before any experiment ran. The question survives as a research direction but needed a sharper formulation and larger scale to be testable.

Thought interleaving at toy scale

CoCoMix-style thought interleaving mechanisms don't produce benefits below ~10M parameters. The mechanism works but the scale is too small to measure what it can do.

Learned byte segmentation at small scale

Learned segmentation boundaries consistently lose to fixed-stride segmentation at the scales we tested. The learning signal isn't strong enough below ~100M parameters. BLT works at 8B, so this may reverse at scale — but not within our compute regime.

Rank as a functional observable under flatten-then-project readouts

Four flat rank-k projection curves across GSM8K-Aug and ProsQA, two distillation regimes, and two thinker settings. Vanilla GPT-2 SFT matches matrix-CODI on ProsQA within 0.26 points. The structural reason: any readout linear in Z has a constant Jacobian and cannot produce rank-aware gradients. Nine architectural fixes killed by the same argument before any GPU commit. Dead under flatten-then-project; survivors require a mechanism no linear map can flatten.
