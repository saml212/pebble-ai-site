The gradient does not see rank: a structural explanation for the Illusion of Superposition in latent chain-of-thought.

Sam Larson

pebble, San Francisco

April 17, 2026  ·  sam@pebbleml.com

abstract

Continuous chain-of-thought models compress reasoning into latent tokens. Matrix-valued variants introduce a new structural observable — the rank of the latent matrix Z. If matrix latents carry parallel reasoning paths via superposition, rank should track them, and truncating Z to low rank should hurt accuracy when the task needs more than k paths. Across four training regimes of a matrix-CODI model (three on ProsQA, one on GSM8K-Aug at a below-threshold operating point), the rank-k projection ablation curve is flat to within 0.6 percentage points. A three-seed replication yields accuracy at 81.51 ± 1.2pp while the final effective rank of Z spans {4, 12, 13}; the loss does not reward any particular rank. To test whether rank-blindness arises from the flatten-then-project readout alone, four readout variants are constructed: a bilinear reparametrization, a bilinear-plus-GELU readout explicitly nonlinear in Z, an SVD-augmented readout feeding singular values through an MLP (computed via torch.linalg.svdvals for numerical stability), and a quadratic readout in the second moment ZZ⊤. All four rank-k curves remain flat (Spearman p values 0.63, 0.14, 0.82, 0.46). Readout linearity is a sufficient but not necessary condition: even readouts nonlinear in Z do not produce rank-dependent curves, indicating the simple Jacobian-linearity mechanism is incomplete. A refined mechanism — an effectively rank-1 active subspace of the trained readout's Jacobian — is deferred to future work. A linear probe on Z (1536 features across 6 positions) underperforms a raw pretrained hidden state (768 features) at target prediction (AUC 0.673 vs 0.846). Rank-k ablation should not be used as a probe for superposition in CODI-trained matrix latents.

01Setup and what was already known

A matrix-CODI model forks CODI [5] and inserts a d × d matrix bottleneck on each latent position's feedback path. Given the previous latent hidden state h ∈ ℝD, the bottleneck maps it up to a d × d matrix, optionally applies a multiplicative thinking step, and projects back to the residual dimension via a readout φ : ℝd×d → ℝD. The default readout is φ(Z) = Wdown · vec(Z) — a flatten-then-project. We use d = 16 throughout, on top of a GPT-2 backbone, and train with the CODI self-distillation objective on ProsQA and GSM8K-Aug.

Why matrix latents. Vector representations are flat: a hidden state of dimension D has D scalar entries with no native notion of how many independent components it superposes. Matrix-valued representations have an additional structural observable — rank, the number of independent directions used. Matrix-valued working memory has a long lineage in neural networks: fast-weight networks (Schmidhuber 1992; Schlag, Irie, Schmidhuber 2021), linear attention (Katharopoulos et al. 2020), the xLSTM family (Beck et al. 2024), and the Mamba/SSM family (Gu & Dao 2023) all use matrix-shaped state, and recent work measures the rank of these states as a diagnostic of how the model uses its memory (Nazari & Rusch 2026; State Rank Dynamics 2026). In all of those, the matrix lives inside a layer — an attention head's accumulator, an SSM's recurrent state — and serves as recurrent memory. Matrix-CODI extends the lineage to a different placement: the matrix sits on the explicit chain-of-thought feedback path, where each latent reasoning position is a d × d matrix rather than a vector. The rank of that matrix is a candidate measure of how many reasoning paths the model holds in superposition at that step. The hypothesis we test is whether rank, so defined, behaves as that measure under CODI-style training.

The superposition hypothesis [3, 4] predicts that continuous latents hold multiple reasoning paths simultaneously. Matrix-valued latents sharpen the hypothesis: each singular direction of Z could encode a separate path. The natural empirical probe is to SVD-truncate Z to rank k before the readout and measure accuracy. A flat accuracy-vs-k curve is evidence that rank is not functional.

Recent empirical work by Rizvi-Martel et al. [8] — the Illusion of Superposition paper — reports that a fine-tuned COCONUT model reaches 96.6% on ProsQA without latent tokens, against 99.0% with them and 85.3% for explicit chain-of-thought. Their result is that the latent machinery is largely decorative in the fine-tuned regime; our contribution is a mechanism at the architecture level that explains, specifically for matrix-valued latents, why the structural observable the superposition hypothesis predicts should matter does not.

02Four flat rank-k curves

We ran the matrix bottleneck under four training conditions that vary the task, the CODI distillation weight γ, and the multiplicative thinker.

Neither the task, the distillation weight, nor the thinker moves the curve. Removing the distillation loss (γ = 0) raises the effective rank from ~10 to ~13, because the training signal is softer, but does not change the shape of the rank-k curve. The functionally used rank of Z, read out by any downstream operation in the stack, is 1.

03The readout Jacobian is constant in Z

The flatten-then-project readout φ(Z) = Wdown · vec(Z) is linear in Z. Its Jacobian

∂φ(Z)/∂Z[i, j] = W_down[:, ij]     (constant in Z)

does not depend on Z. The loss gradient with respect to Z is a vector contracted with a constant tensor. It cannot depend on the singular structure of Z, and the optimizer has no training signal that rewards rank. This argument applies to any readout linear in Z — Frobenius inner products, low-rank factored linear projections, per-vocab bilinear logits — all of which have constant Jacobians.

This is a sufficient condition for rank-blindness. The rest of the paper asks whether it is also necessary.

04Three-seed accuracy-rank decoupling

If the loss does not reward rank, two training seeds of the same configuration should be allowed to land at different ranks while reaching comparable accuracy. We ran three seeds (1337, 42, 7) of the flatten-readout configuration on ProsQA with γ = 0, 25 epochs, batch 16, AdamW at lr = 10−4.

The same loss lands at effective ranks spanning a 3× range, at matching accuracy. This directly demonstrates that the matrix-bottleneck training objective is rank-agnostic — stronger than the flat rank-k curve alone, because it shows the loss landscape is flat along rank-changing directions of Z.

result 1 Three training seeds of the same configuration yield ProsQA accuracies 80.47%, 81.25%, 82.81% (81.51 ± 1.2pp) while converging to final effective ranks 12.9, 4, 12 — a 3× spread in rank at matching accuracy. Rank is a free direction in the loss landscape.

05Positive control: four readouts nonlinear in Z

The Jacobian argument in §3 gives a sufficient condition for rank-blindness. The natural prediction is that a readout with non-constant Jacobian — a readout explicitly nonlinear in Z — should bend the rank-k curve. We tested four variants, replacing only the readout φ and leaving everything else (γ=0, d = 16, six latent positions, seed 1337, 25 epochs, batch 16) identical.

  • Bilinear. φ(Z) = W · [uk⊤ Z vk]k=1..d². Reparametrization of flatten. Still linear in Z. Serves as a control.
  • Bilinear + GELU. φ(Z) = W · GELU([uk⊤ Z vk]). Nonlinear in Z.
  • SVD-augmented. φ(Z) = Wdown · vec(Z) + MLP(σ(Z)) where σ(Z) are the singular values of Z. Explicitly exposes rank.
  • Quadratic. φ(Z) = Wdown · vec(concat(Z Z⊤, Z⊤ Z)). Second-moment readout. Quadratic in Z.
result 2 The Jacobian hypothesis predicts that a nonlinear-in-Z readout (bilinear+GELU, SVD-augmented, quadratic) should bend the rank-k curve. None of them do. The flat rank-k curve is not specific to readout linearity — it persists through readouts whose Jacobians are demonstrably non-constant. Readout linearity is a sufficient but not necessary condition: the matrix-bottleneck training objective produces rank-indifferent gradients through the full chain rule.

A candidate refined hypothesis: the trained readout's Jacobian at test inputs has an effectively rank-1 active subspace in Z, regardless of whether the readout is in-principle nonlinear. This would explain why even SVD-augmented readouts (which explicitly expose singular values to the optimizer via torch.linalg.svdvals) produce flat curves — the backward pass is numerically stable, and the empirical result (SVD-aug best accuracy 78.12%, below flatten at 80.47%) rules out the failure mode of the optimizer silently zeroing the SVD branch. The Jacobian-row-space effective rank measurement is a camera-ready experiment; the refined hypothesis is not yet tested by this submission.

06Depth and scale do not rescue matrix-CODI

Two natural follow-up questions are whether the failure is specific to six latent positions and whether it persists at larger scale. We ran a depth sweep (vanilla CODI with iterative refinement) and a scale sweep (vanilla SFT and matrix-CODI at gpt2-small, gpt2-medium, gpt2-large).

Two observations. First, adding iterative latent refinement at n = 6 hurts ProsQA accuracy — the "more latent thought means better reasoning" story fails at this scale independently of the matrix question. Second, matrix-CODI tracks below vanilla SFT at both gpt2-small and gpt2-medium; we do not have the large matrix point yet. Vanilla SFT itself degrades at gpt2-large on ProsQA — we read that as a data-size artefact of ProsQA (17,886 training examples) under default AdamW at the larger backbone, not as a finding about superposition. Matrix-CODI does not rescue the regression.

07What does Z encode, if not rank?

If rank is not functional, we should ask what is encoded in the matrix thought. We ran a 5-fold cross-validated multi-class logistic regression to predict the ProsQA target class from a flattened Z on 500 held-out test problems. Controls: the same prompt's hidden state from a pretrained GPT-2 with no ProsQA fine-tuning, and the same model at random initialization.

result 3 The matrix-CODI bottleneck's Z concatenated across 6 positions (1536 features) reaches AUC 0.673 at predicting the ProsQA target class. Vanilla pretrained GPT-2, never trained on ProsQA, reaches 0.846 at 768 features. Despite having more features, the matrix bottleneck does not deliver target-predictive information commensurate with its feature count. A dimension-matched comparison is deferred to camera-ready.

08Positioning vs recent adjacent work

Two 2026 February preprints measure rank in trained transformer states.

Nazari & Rusch [9] measure the effective rank of linear-attention fast-weight hidden states — the d × d memory inside a linear attention layer — and propose post-training rank pruning of K and Q matrices. Their object of study is the fast-weight memory, not an explicit CoT latent, and their claim is descriptive: they observe rank structure, report a distribution, and prune.

State Rank Dynamics [10] reports a "state-rank stratification" phenomenon during pretraining: linear-attention heads bifurcate into persistently low-rank and persistently high-rank groups. Object: fast-weight memory. Claim type: descriptive.

This paper differs on two axes. First, the object of study: we measure rank in explicit matrix latent tokens on the feedback path of a matrix-CODI model, not in linear-attention fast-weight memories. Second, the claim type: we make a mechanism claim about the training objective (§3) and we test it by falsification using four nonlinear-in-Z positive controls (§5). A descriptive paper cannot make that claim. We are not the first to empirically measure rank in trained transformer states; those two papers do that for linear-attention states. We are the first, to our knowledge, to diagnose rank-blindness as a property of the readout-plus-objective and to falsifiably test that diagnosis.

Three additional papers are relevant. Wang et al. (arXiv:2510.15522) propose latent reasoning is a superposition in vocabulary column space, not in Z's SVD basis — a distinct substrate our rank-k ablation does not test. Fan et al. (CVPR 2025, arXiv:2411.07635) show rank augmentation is achievable in linear attention for vision; our negative result is scoped to the readout-plus-objective family tested here. Li & Janson (NeurIPS 2024, arXiv:2409.09951) show that zero/resample ablations overestimate component importance relative to optimal ablation; this cuts in our favor (optimal ablation would flatten curves further) and is the right methodological citation for the rank-k probe.

SIM-CoT [6] (Shen et al., ICLR 2026) diagnoses latent CoT instability as insufficient step-level supervision and proposes injecting per-step targets. Our diagnosis — rank-indifferent gradients from the CODI distillation objective — is different. The two are compatible and non-redundant: if SIM-CoT's diagnosis were the whole story, fixing the readout's Jacobian would shift which component of Z the gradient shapes; it would not eliminate rank-dependence entirely. Our positive controls falsify the simplest readout-only fix and are consistent with both mechanisms operating simultaneously.

Illusion of Superposition [8] reports the behavioral finding that fine-tuned latent reasoners do not need their latents on ProsQA (96.6% without, 99.0% with). We do not reproduce their number (our vanilla SFT reaches 81.77%), and the structural argument in §3 is independent of matching it. The two findings sit parallel: they establish that the latents are empirically not doing work; we establish that, specifically for matrix-valued latents trained with CODI distillation, the rank observable cannot do work even in principle, through any readout we tested.

09Limitations

  • Single task family at the main operating point. All core experiments use ProsQA. GSM8K-Aug shows a flat rank-k curve at 6% accuracy, a low-accuracy regime where the model is barely learning the task; we do not read it as strong standalone evidence. A higher-accuracy GSM8K cross-dataset run is queued.
  • Single architecture family. All runs on GPT-2. The structural mechanism is architecture-agnostic, but the empirical evidence covers only GPT-2 small/medium/large.
  • One seed per positive control. The four readout variants in §5 were trained once. Seed variation across the three-seed flatten replication is 1.2 pp, well below the room within which a non-flat curve would announce itself, so we do not expect the qualitative picture to change; but a three-seed per-variant run would narrow confidence intervals.
  • Effective rank, not exact rank. We report effective rank (exp of singular-value entropy) and use hard SVD truncation for the ablation. The conclusion is robust to this choice — the ablation directly tests whether the rest of Z's spectrum carries functional information — but readers should note the distinction.
  • Seed-dependent Z rank is itself a finding. The 3× rank spread at matched accuracy (Fig. 1) is not just a consistency check on the rank-k ablation; it is a distinct empirical observation worth exploring in its own right. A designer who wants a controlled-rank Z likely cannot get it without changing the objective.
